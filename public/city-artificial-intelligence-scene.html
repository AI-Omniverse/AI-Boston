Facts About Boston's
AI Ecosystem

Key figures, data, and statistics
about Boston as an AI hub.


Strengths

  • Biotech
  • Surgical Robots
  • Pharmaceutical
  • Healthcare
  • Education
  • Software
  • Research

Outlook Boston’s identity is closely tied to its universities, hospitals, and research institutions, and students and scientists from around the world fight for the opportunity to move there to study and work. This constant flow of new top-tier ideas and talent creates an environment where advanced technologies in AI and robotics will continue to quickly grow.


Snapshot

  • The Greater Boston area has more than 75 colleges and universities with AI-related programs creating one of the largest talent pipelines in the world.
  • Harvard's innovation labs have helped launch 4,000+ startups, while MIT's "The Engine" startup accelerator has raised $5.5 billion in funding for deep-tech startups.
  • Boston is home to over 250 robotics companies topping the list of American cities.
  • The biotech cluster in Greater Boston includes over 1,000 life science companies utilizing machine learning and AI for drug discovery, medical imaging, and genomic analysis.
  • Startups at MassRobotics, the world's largest robotics hub, have collectively raised more than $2 billion in venture funding.
